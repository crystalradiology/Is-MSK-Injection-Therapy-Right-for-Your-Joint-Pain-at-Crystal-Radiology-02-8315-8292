What is MSK Injection Therapy?
Musculoskeletal (MSK) injection therapy is a specialised treatment used to manage pain and inflammation in joints, muscles, tendons, and surrounding tissues.
The treatment involves delivering targeted injections directly into the affected area, helping to reduce pain and improve function. These injections may include anti-inflammatory medications or other therapeutic agents, depending on your condition.
Patients across Australia are increasingly choosing MSK Injections at Crystal Radiology because they offer a non-surgical solution with effective and lasting results.
How Does MSK Injection Therapy Work?
MSK injections work by delivering medication precisely where it is needed most—into the joint or soft tissue causing discomfort.
Here’s how the process typically works:
•	The affected area is carefully assessed using imaging guidance (such as ultrasound)
•	A fine needle is used to inject medication directly into the target area
•	The medication reduces inflammation and relieves pain
•	Over time, patients experience improved movement and joint function
•	Because the treatment is image-guided, it ensures accuracy and maximises the effectiveness of the injection.

Common Conditions Treated with MSK Injection Therapy
MSK injections are used to treat a variety of musculoskeletal conditions affecting joints and soft tissues.
Conditions that may benefit include:
•	Arthritis (including osteoarthritis)
•	Bursitis (inflammation of fluid-filled sacs around joints)
•	Tendonitis (inflamed tendons)
•	Chronic knee pain
•	Shoulder pain and stiffness
•	Hip joint discomfort
•	Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis)
•	Ankle instability or pain
•	Joint inflammation
•	Persistent musculoskeletal pain
If you are experiencing any of these conditions, your doctor may recommend MSK Injections at Crystal Radiology as part of your treatment plan.
